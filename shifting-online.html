Shifting to Online Channels

Building an online presence will significantly soften the blow from the reduction in physical interaction

There are many different paths that can be taken to increase your business' online presence, depending on its industry

  • RESTURANTS
  • RETAIL
  • SERVICES
Online Avenues for Resturants

  1. Sign up for DoorDash, Uber Eats, or GrubHub and compare the different benefits they offer. Choose what is right for your business.
  2. Follow the guidelines on their respective websites for joining their service.
  3. Make sure your customers know about your new delivery option through sales, social media promotions, emails, and more.
Investing in an online method of selling goods is not only necessary for the COVID-19 induced restrictions, but is also important as it helps your business adapt to changing business realities of the future.

Online Avenues for Retail Businesses

  1. Set up an e-commerce presence. This can happen in two ways. E-commerce will help bypass the seller and allow you to keep a higher portion of the profits for your business.
    • Utilize online resources such WordPress, Shopify, GoDaddy, or many others to create your own e-commerce store.
    • Hire a professional agency to design your website for you
    Both are good choices; a decision should be made based off of the position of your company.
  2. Ensure a delivery mechanism is set up:
    • This can include getting boxes, packaging tape, etc., and training staff in packaging and sending out orders.
    • This also includes finding a provider such as UPS or Fedex to do your business deliveries, as well as integrating their system with your business.
  3. Ensure that your website is optimized for search engines. By doing this, it is more likely a potential customer will choose your website over another. There are many services online that can help with this:
    • What is SEO?
    • Moz
    • Search Engine Land

Online Avenues for Services

Not all services are able to be conducted online. However, those that are should take advantage of e-commerce. Businesses encompass a whole host of industries that do not require physical interaction with customers. Lawyers, real estate agents, and tutors can all utilize online platforms to interact with customers and manage workflow from home.

  • Skype
  • Zoom
Investing in an online method of selling goods is not only necessary for COVID-19 induced restrictions, but is also important on its own, as it helps your business adapt to changing business realities of the future.
