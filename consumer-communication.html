Consumer Communication

Communicating with your customers doesn't need to be difficult even when physical interaction is discouraged.

There are a many ways to keep people aware of your brand by utilizing the internet.

  • Establishing a social media presence

    Other than creating email chains with customers that have already uploaded their emails into a rewards program or made an account with the business, companies should also be creating social media platforms, such as Instagram, to communicate certain health initiatives that they are taking to better meet the needs of their customers. Actively communicating this information is vital, as it makes consumers feel more comfortable purchasing goods or relying on the services provided by the business as they would know that measures are being taken to keep products and workers healthy and safe.

    In addition to including updates about new health initiatives taking place, a social media platform would also be a good place for businesses to promote new products or services.

  • Community Involvement

    Expressing involvement within the community shows that the business is not only making reforms within their own walls, but in other areas as well. Staying involved in the community can take numerous forms, including but not limited to:

    • Actively taking part in donations to a non-profits or NGOs: Businesses could also donate a certain percentage of the proceeds they earn in a designated time frame to a local homeless shelter.
