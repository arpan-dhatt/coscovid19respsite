Health Information

Learn what COVID-19 is and how to prevent its spread

Due to the ongoing nature of the COVID-19 pandemic, there aren't any medications, such as vaccines and antivirals, that can prevent or cure it. However, there are many steps you can take to prevent or mitigate its spread.

FAQs about COVID-19

  • What is COVID-19?
    COVID-19 is a highly contagious respiratory illness with sustained community transmission in several countries, including the United States(majority of cases are not imported). The official name of of the virus is "severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2"(SARS-CoV-2). It is currently theorized the virus originated in Wuhan, China in November 2019.
  • How does COVID-19 spread?

    It is believed that COVID-19 spreads mainly between people who are in close contact with one another (within 6 feet of each other) via small airborne droplets that are produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes. A person can also get COVID-19 by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it and then touching their face.

  • What are the symptoms of COVID-19?

    Most people who have COVID-19 will experience mild symptoms similar to those of the common cold or flu. Some people may not experience any symptoms at all. The most common symptoms include but are not limited to:

    • Fever
    • Dry Cough
    • Shortness of Breath

    However, some people will experience more severe symptoms that can be life threatening. If someone experiences these symptoms they should call their health care provider immediately:

    • Pneumonia
    • Multiple Organ Failure

  • Who is affected by the coronavirus?

    Most people will survive their infection without requiring any medical treatment. On the contrary, there are some people who are more vulnerable than others.

    • People with respiratory illnesses or compromised immune systems are especially susceptible to developing severe complications from COVID-19. Furthermore, elderly people (those above the age of 60) are far more likely to develop severe symptoms that require hospitalization.
    • It is important that everyone takes proper precautions to prevent the spread of the virus in order to protect these vulnerable demographics.

  • What can I do to prevent the spread of COVID-19?

    There are many precautions you can take to mitigate the spread of COVID-19. Most of these are standard hygiene practices that apply to many diseases:

    • Avoid close contact with people who are currently sick.
    • Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth with unwashed hands.
    • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. Use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer that contains at least 60% alcohol if soap and water are not available.

  • What should I do if I have symptoms?

    Symptoms can appear 2-14 days after you have been exposed to the virus. If you are experiencing any symptoms it is advised to:

    • Self-Quarantine: Avoid going to public places.
    • Cover your cough or sneeze with a tissue, then dispose of the tissue in the trash.
    • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces such as doorknobs and counters.

    If you start experiencing the below warning signs get medical attention immediately. These warning signs include but are not limited to:

    • Trouble breathing
    • Peristent pain or pressure in the chest
    • New confusion or inability to arouse
    • Bluish lips or face

  • Is there a treatment for COVID-19?

    Currently, there is no specific antiviral treatment for COVID-19. People with COVID-19 can seek medical care only to help relieve symptoms.

FAQs for Small Businesses During the Pandemic

  • What can I currently do to keep safe as a small business owner?

    • Encourage sick employees to stay at home until they have been fever-free for 24 hours without taking any fever reduction medicines. Encourage minimal contact between employees, as well as proper hand washing hygiene (20 seconds).
    • Do not require a doctor’s note for employees who are sick to confirm their illness or to return to work. Many doctor’s offices and medical facilities will be extremely busy during this time and may not be able to provide timely documentation. If an employee is not feeling well, actively encourage them to stay home.
    • If an employee shows symptoms of a respiratory illness, make sure they are separated from other employees and sent home immediately. Make sure they follow proper respiratory hygiene by using tissues when coughing or sneezing, as well as washing hands often.
    • Routinely perform a thorough cleaning of surfaces that are frequently touched, such as doorknobs and counters. Visible efforts at disinfection can leave a positive impression with customers.
    • If an employee is confirmed to have COVID-19, employers should inform fellow employees of their possible exposure to COVID-19 in the workplace. Employers should ensure that they maintain the confidentiality of the diagnosed employee by adhering to the guidelines set by the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).

  • What can I do to plan for the future as a small business owner

    • Develop a plan to continue business operations with a reduced number of employees. Absenteeism due to the virus can be planned for by ensuring all essential business functions can continue with a reduced workforce.
      • Train other personnel to perform essential functions in case other key team members are absent.
      • Employees should play a key role in developing the plan.
    • Consider establishing flexible worksites (telecommuting) and/or flexible work hours (staggered shifts with cleanings in between) to increase the physical distance among employees in order to adhere to local health guidelines.
    • Plan for how your business might operate should there be an increase in absenteeism among employees, or an interruption within supply chains. This might include considering how the following might be impacted by a sustained outbreak of COVD-19:
      • Due to school closure in the area, some employees may be forced to care for sick relatives or children. Plan accordingly
    • Keep employees well informed, and anticipate any fear, anxiety, rumors, or misinformation that may be spreading due to COVID-19.
