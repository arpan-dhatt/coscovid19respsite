health-steps-title
Frequently Asked Questions
==============================
health-steps-1-title
What is COVID-19?
==============================
health-steps-1-body
The official name for COVID-19 is "severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2"(SARS-CoV-2). The virus was first identified in Wuhan, China in 2019, and has since spread globally, resulting in the 2019-20 coronavirus pandemic. It is a member of the coronavirus family of viruses and shares similarities to the common cold virus(a rhinovirus) and influenza.
==============================
health-steps-2-title
How does COVID-19 spread?
==============================
health-steps-2-body
It is believed to spread mainly between people who are in close contact with one another (within 6 feet of each other) via small airborne droplets that are produced when an infected person coughs or sneezes. A person can also get COVID-19 by touching a surface or object that has the virus on it and then touching their face.
==============================
health-steps-3-title
What are the symptoms of COVID-19?
==============================
health-steps-3-body
Most COVID-19 patients have mild symptoms:
<ul>
<li>Fever</li>
<li>Dry Cough></li>
<li>Shortness of breath</li>
</ul>
However, some people can have more severe reactions to the virus and require medical attention. These symptoms include viral pneuomonia and multiple organ failure.
==============================
health-steps-4-title
Who is affected by the novel coronavirus?
==============================
health-steps-4-body
People with respiratory illnesses and compromised immune systems are the most susceptible to severe symptoms. This vulnerable group includes elderly people, who are most at risk. Younger people who have a healthy immune system without any other complications are likely to survive the virus with mild symptoms similar to that of the flu or cold.

It is important for everyone, no matter how vulnerable, to practice proper hygiene, such as washing hands, in order to protect the people who are at the most risk.
==============================
health-steps-5-title
What can I do to help contain COVID-19?
==============================
health-steps-5-body
There are many ways to contain the virus:
<ul>
<li>Avoid close contact with people showing symptoms of infection.</li>
<li>Avoid touching your face(eyes, nose, mouth) without washing your hands.</li>
<li>Wash your hands often with soap AND water for at least 20 seconds. Use an alcohol-based hand-sanitizer(antibiotic ones will not work against viruses) that contains at least 60% rubbing alcohol if soap and water are not available.</li>
</ul>
==============================
health-steps-6-title
What should I do if I have symptoms of COVID-19?
==============================
health-steps-6-body
There are no anti-viral treatments design to fight COVID-19. However, there steps you can take to prevent spreading the virus:
<ul>
<li>Practice Social Distancing: Stay 6 feet away from people at all times and avoid bodily contact(hugs, handshakes).</li>
<li>Self-Quarantine: Stay at home as often as possible and do not go to work.</li>
<li>Disinfect surfaces that are commonly touched, such as doornobs, counters, computer keyboards, mice, and cell phones.</li>
<li>Cover your sneeze when you cough to prevent droplets from staying a long time in the air and settling on surfaces. Dispose of any tissues used in the trash.</li>54544545454545454544545
</ul>
==============================
health-steps-7-title
Are there any treatments for COVID-19?
==============================
health-steps-7-body
There are no vaccines or anti-viral medications for COVID-19. Currently, symptoms are the only thing that can be treated. However, it is advised to NOT take excessive amounts of anti-histamines since they will suppress your immune system. If you have serious systems contact your health care provider and they will advise you.
==============================
health-steps-8-title
What can I do to stay safe as a small business owner?
==============================
health-steps-8-body
<ul>
<li>Encourage sick employees to stay at home until they have been fever-free for 24 hours without taking any fever reduction medicines. Encourage minimal contact between employees, as well as proper hand washing hygiene (20 seconds).</li>
<li>Do not require a doctor’s note for employees who are sick to confirm their illness or to return to work. Many doctor’s offices and medical facilities will be extremely busy during this time and may not be able to provide timely documentation. If an employee is not feeling well, actively encourage them to stay home.
</li>
<li>If an employee shows symptoms of a respiratory illness, make sure they are separated from other employees and sent home immediately. Make sure they follow proper respiratory hygiene by using tissues when coughing or sneezing, as well as washing hands often.
</li>
<li>Routinely perform a thorough cleaning of surfaces that are frequently touched, such as doorknobs and counters.  Visible efforts at disinfection can leave a positive impression with customers.</li>
<li></li>
</ul>
