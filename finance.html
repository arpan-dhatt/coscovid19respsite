Finance Information

Learn about financial changes made due to COIVID-19 and how you business can adapt to them

The drop in revenue casued by COVID-19 is casuing issues for businesses of all sizes. Due to this, the government and other organizations have changed policies and immplemented some new programs to help businesses cope. It's important to see whats out there and take advantage of those programs to help your business get through these trying times.

Finance

  • Cashflow, Loans

    Businesses still have existing obligations to meet, despite the reduction in sales caused by COVID-19. Although not an ideal solution, it is recommended that small businesses take low-interest loans from the US Small Business Administration in order to cover obligations for the next few months until the economic consequence of COVID-19 is lessened.

    • The federal government recently enabled the Economic Injury Disaster Loan Program in Texas. Under this program, businesses are eligible for a low-interest loan of up to 2 million dollars to cover working capital needs.
    • If other funding is needed, the Small Business Administrations Lender Match program can be used to compare different lenders and access a SBA approved loan.
  • Taxes

    The federal government has deferred federal income tax payments for entities other than C-Corporations until June 15th, 2020. This does not mean that taxes are to be filed by June 15th -- April 15th, 2020 remains the filing deadline for tax returns.

    For C-Corporations, income tax payment deadlines are being automatically extended until July 15th, 2020, for up to $10 million of their 2019 tax due.

  • Cashflow, Payments

    It is important to reach out to lenders and other entities to which your business has a financial obligation in order to see if payment structures can be reworked.

    • Many lenders, such as Capital One, Citi, and Wells Fargo have all issued statements indicating that they are willing to work with customers that experience financial difficulties. For example, Citi has waived service charges for small businesses for 30 days.
    • It is important that your business reaches out to its lenders to see if they either have programs in place to help small businesses or to contact customer service to negotiate terms.
