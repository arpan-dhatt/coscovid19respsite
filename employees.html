Employees Information

Learn how to best utilize employees in a time of decreased demand and how to help them overcome adversity caused by COVID-19

On the business side, a few steps can be taken to ensure that your business is able to utilize and hold on to your employees.

  • Remote Working

    The CDC and WHO recommend that businesses shift towards remote working in order to continue the policy of social distancing and to slow the spread of the virus.

    There are numerous services and tools that businesses can use in order to make a seamless transition to remote working.

    • Chat/Instant Messaging
      • Slack and Microsoft Teams are business focused communication platforms that allow for communication between different teams, departments, and the company as a whole. They have different channels that allow for communication to occur between different segments of the company, and are currently used by many large businesses.
    • Virtual Interaction
      • Platforms like Google Hangouts and Zoom allow for virtual face-to-face interaction between members of your company, but also with members of other companies. These platforms also come with features like screen-sharing capabilities, recording, and live chat, which can aid in communicating remotely.
    • Cloud Storage
      • It can be difficult for employees to access their company's internal server and data when they are not in the office, which is where cloud storage comes in. Platforms such as Google Drive or Dropbox allow the seamless sharing and data accessing from anywhere, and can play a pivotal role in enabling remote working.
    • Productivity Trackers
      • It can be easy to get off track and distracted when at home, but fortunately, there are a whole host of productivity trackers that businesses can use to hold employees accountable and ensure they are not getting off task. Some popular productivity trackers are Toggl, Todoist and Focus Booster.
    • Project Management Tools
      • Maintaining a normal workflow and communicating everyone's roles and tasks can be challenging when working remotely. That is why it is important to use project management tools so each employee knows what to do and when. They also enable management to have better oversight over how employees are functioning. Some good project management tools are Trello, Basecamp, Asana, Airtable or Monday.com. These applications allow managers to view both lists of pre-planned goals and steps in order to achieve them, all to ensure that even when working remotely, a clear agenda for all employees is set.
  • Alternative Task/Company Development

    Although normal business has been disrupted because of the virus, it does not mean that employees automatically become redundant. This time can be used for a variety of functions that can better prepare businesses for the future, once COVID-19 has passed.

    Employees can work on

    • Marketing Teams:
      • Look out for new opportunities amongst this adversity
      • Consumer outreach through email updates and social media
      • Skills training
    • Management:
      • Skills training
      • Develop new strategies for sales, customer service, etc.
      • Developing stronger internal management systems
    • Lower-level workers:
      • Skills training
      • Repairs, maintenance, or renovations
      • Stock up on products, services, etc. for after the outbreak passes
  • Meeting Payroll

    The reduction in cash flow means that it will be hard for business to maintain payroll, but there are a few steps that can be taken in order to mitigate this issue.

    • Cut working hours or obligations to justify a temporary lower salary.
    • Encourage employees to take their vacation days now.
    • Furlough employees and ask them to take a temporary pay cut.

COVID-19 has caused a huge amount of uncertainty and stress for employees whose jobs and health may now be at risk. It is important that businesses are there to help deal with these concerns on the employee side for long term sustainability.

  • Employee Anxiety/Uncertainty

    The uncertainty caused by a decrease in business and lack of knowledge about COVID-19 can cause great anxiety and fear in employees. It is important for businesses to take steps to reduce this fear for the well-being of the nation, and for the efficiency and success of their business. Here are a few steps that can be taken to reduce this issue.

    • Communicate frequently and openly with your employees, ensuring they are in the loop of communication to erase any sense of uncertainty.
    • Implement proper hygiene and health recommendations from the CDC and educate employees about the steps your business is taking to protect their health. More information about health steps that can be taken can be found here
    • Try to be flexible in working accommodations. Allow employees to work remotely or at their own pace to deal with school closures and other exigent restrictions put in place to deal with the virus.
    • Limit employee travel and exposure to areas that could expose them to the virus.
    • Make sure to communicate and implement new recommendations/restrictions the government has imposed in order to fight the spread of COVID-19.
    • Create an environment that lets employees know they can communicate their concerns or queries to management.
  • Medical Leave
    • The “Families First” Coronavirus Aid Package has made some changes to the status of medical leave for employees.
    • If an employee is unable to work because they are subject to quarantine or isolation, experiencing symptoms of COVID–19, caring for someone who is in quarantine or isolation, and/or have children in schools that have closed, employers with fewer than 500 employees must provide two weeks' worth of paid sick leave.
    • Employers themselves will receive tax credits to offset the costs of providing this paid leave.
    • It is important to allow employees to be healthy at home or allow them to help others at home to ensure their productivity and loyalty to the company after this disease subsides.
  • Employee Finance

    Many employees are now struggling to afford their former cost of living (bills, rent, food, and other essentials) due to reduced hours and furloughs from the virus.

    This is harmful, as employees make up the foundation of any business and will be essential to the recovery of a business after the pandemic subsides.It is hard to find loyal and hardworking employees, so it is important to take steps to retain them. A company can do this in many ways.

    • Talk with employees and provide resources that better enable them to budget their money and get by during these hard times.
    • Encourage employees to find jobs elsewhere, in industries that now require manpower, such as:
      • Delivery Drivers
      • Warehouse workers
Some companies have decided to offer free services to small businesses to help them weather this outbreak. They could be worth checking out.
  • Surfshark VPN is giving free 6-month accounts to companies with less than ten employees to their VPN service.
  • Cisco is extending services for Webex, its video conference platform for free accounts. Their offer includes unlimited usage of the platform without time restriction and support for accounts with fewer than 100 participants.
  • Cloudflare is offering their Cloudflare for Teams platform for free through September 1st to small businesses. Their tool allows remote workers to connect securely and easily to corporate apps. They are also providing a free session to help business owners get the platform set up.
  • GoToMeeting is offering 3 months of free site-wide licensing of its video conferencing platform for eligible organizations (health care providers, educational institutions, municipalities and non-profits).
  • Microsoft is providing a 6-month free trial of Office 365 E1, including Microsoft Teams, its collaboration and video conferencing platform.
