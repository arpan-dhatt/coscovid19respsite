Sales Maintenance Information

Communicating with your customers and selling to them doesn't need to be difficult even when physical interaction is discouraged.

There are many things that can be done to adapt to the changing atmosphere caused by COVID-19.

There are many things that can be done to maintain sales in this business changing landscapre

  • Payment deferrals

    Many individuals see themselves furloughed or with reduced hours for the foreseeable future, however, they still maintain their jobs and will continue to earn their normal wages once this crisis blows over. Thus, enabling them to simply defer payments has less risk and the benefit of maintaining sales in this economic downturn.

    Payment deferals can look like allowing customers to pay in multiple installments, or allowing them to pay the full cost of a product in a few months once the pandemic had been dealt with.

  • Switching to E-commerce

    Consumers have significantly changed the way in which they purchase goods due to the various travel restrictions and advisories in place. Many have switched to purchasing from e-commerce sites to continue the policy of social distancing. In order for your business to remain competitive and widen its range, it is imperative the internet is used for sales.

    See here for a guide on how to move your business online depending on your industry.

    In addition, this switch has two main benefits.

    • Firstly, it allows the staff of the business to remain productive in an environment in which they might not have other things to do.
    • Secondly, it has the added benefit of setting up a strong foundation for growth after COVID-19 passes. It has already emerged as a trend that individuals are shifting away from brick-and-mortar to e-commerce platforms, and crises in the past have shown that COVID-19 is only set to increase the consumers’ acceptance and use of online channels of business.
  • Utilizing delivery services

    Restaurants specifically should look to delivery services such as Uber Eats, Grubhub, and DoorDash to maintain sales. These services have seen a 56% rise in use over the past two weeks - consumers still want to eat out, but cannot leave the house due to COVID-19. Offering food delivery solves this issue and also sets up your restaurant for future growth once this crisis ends.

    See here for a guide on how to move your business online depending on your industry.

  • Switch to Online Service Platforms

    Although not possible for all service sectors, it may be an option to send your service to the customer via the internet through platforms such as Google Hangouts, Microsoft teams, or gotomeeting, etc. Some of these platforms are currently offering free promotions specifically targeted at small businesses to ensure a smooth transition from traditional to remote working options. Although not a substitute for face-to-face interaction, it allows you to retain business and shows customers that you are working hard to persevere through the crisis.

Marketing at a time such as this can be extremely difficult but there are many avenues you can take to reduce the impact on your sales.

  • Sell gift cards

    Provide gift cards to ensure continued sales. Consider providing a discount for future purchases made with gift cards to encourage continued purchases right now.

    Gift cards have the added benefit of providing immediate cash flow to help fund any impending obligations.

  • Set up a rewards system

    Setting up a reward system would encourage customers to continue shopping at your company, establishing a loyal consumer base even after the end of COVID-19.

  • Build a website

    Increasing your online presence has been an important part of marketing for some time, but now it is more important than ever. Fewer people are shopping physically due to the pandemic and are instead resorting to online shopping. Building a website will give people a place to interact with your company during quarantine.

    • Make sure not to focus only on design when creating a website. The reason why small business websites often do not create a return on investment is because they are often more focused on looks rather than sales principles.
    • Make it very, very easy to do one thing - schedule an appointment, buy a product, make a reservation, etc. Too many options or too much complexity can confuse customers.
    • Do not make a website “just so you can have one”. Make it work for you - otherwise, it is useless.
    • Some platforms that may be helpful in creating an effective website that fulfills your needs are Wix, Squarespace, and Site123.

  • Have sales

    Sales and discounts are a proven manner in which you can encourage customers to purchase goods or services. Utilizing them now is as important as ever.

    • They allow customers to get acquainted with new online methods of doing business.
    • It also enables customers to see that your business is still functioning once they make a purchase and is there to help them, instilling confidence in the customer. This can be crucial for customer retention and future sales, both in this time of crisis and afterwards.

  • Reach out to your customers

    Check email lists, mailing lists, and other sources of information about customers and send them marketing material to drive sales from sources that have proven to be open to your brand in the past.

    Utilize social media to better target and advertise to your customers. Learn more about how to use social media here. This also has the added benefit of setting your business up for success in the future once this outbreak passes due to the changing demographics and consumers sentiments.

There are a many ways to keep people aware of your brand by utilizing the internet.

  • Establishing a social media presence

    Other than creating email chains with customers that have already uploaded their emails into a rewards program or made an account with the business, companies should also be creating social media platforms, such as Instagram, to communicate certain health initiatives that they are taking to better meet the needs of their customers. Actively communicating this information is vital, as it makes consumers feel more comfortable purchasing goods or relying on the services provided by the business as they would know that measures are being taken to keep products and workers healthy and safe.

    In addition to including updates about new health initiatives taking place, a social media platform would also be a good place for businesses to promote new products or services.

  • Community Involvement

    Expressing involvement within the community shows that the business is not only making reforms within their own walls, but in other areas as well. Staying involved in the community can take numerous forms, including but not limited to:

    • Actively taking part in donations to a non-profits or NGOs: Businesses could also donate a certain percentage of the proceeds they earn in a designated time frame to a local homeless shelter. This is similar to when local clubs seek funding, aka gaining a portion of a business’s profits made in an allotted time frame.
    • If your business is in the food service industry, you could participate in or sponsor a food drive along with other local businesses.

    These actions could be shared on the social media platform discussed above, and would create a collective and communal sense of trust towards the business. Consumers are more willing to buy from businesses they believe care about the well-being of their customers and community, particularly during times of crisis.
