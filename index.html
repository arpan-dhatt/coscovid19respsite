How Can Your Business Deal With COVID-19?

The City of Southlake is aware of the pressure the coronavirus pandemic is asserting on businesses, especially smaller, local ones. We are commited to providing businesses with all the resources they need to survive this pandemic. This website was created as a resource to provide pertinent information to help busineses adapt to the changing conditions stemming from the COVID-19 outbreak.
Health Information

Due to the ongoing nature of the COVID-19 pandemic, there aren't any medications, such as vaccines and antivirals, that can prevent or cure it. However, there are many steps you can take to prevent or mitigate its spread.

Government Assistance During the Pandemic

Multiple levels of government are putting policies in place to assist businesses. Some of the latest updates are below:

  • LOCAL
  • STATE
  • NATIONAL
Local Policy Changes
  • Tarrant County has updated its Declaration of Local Disaster Due to Public Health Emergency order. The order’s changes are effective, March 21, 2020, at 6:00 p.m. Click here to read more.
  • Following the closure of city facilities to walk-in public access, the City of Southlake has created a centralized portal for online services and quick reference phone lists. Click here to visit.
  • Mayor Laura Hill has issued a Declaration of Disaster as a result of the COVID-19 outbreak. Click to view a video message from Mayor Laura Hill and to read more on how it affects businesses.
State Policy Changes
  • Governor Greg Abbott announced March 20 that the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA) has included the entire state of Texas in its Economic Injury Disaster Declaration and granted access to its Economic Injury Disaster Loan (EIDL) program, which will provide long-term, low-interest loans to qualifying businesses across the state. For additional information on Texas business-related COVID-19 resources, including a list of required documentation for the SBA Loan process, click here.
  • Governor Greg Abbott issued a waiver that will allow restaurants to deliver alcoholic beverages with food purchases to patrons, including beer, wine, and mixed drinks. Click here for the details on this temporary waiver.
National Policy Changes
  • President Trump signed the second COVID-19 legislative response package following Senate passage. An analysis by Thorn Run Partners (TRP), a government affairs firm, of the bill can be found by clicking here. A breakdown of the paid leave provisions can be found by clicking here.
  • The first effort, an $8.3 billion emergency supplemental appropriations bill, cleared both the House of Representatives and Senate and was signed into law on March 6th. An analysis by TRP can be found by clicking here.
  • The Trump administration, spearheaded by Treasury Secretary Mnuchin, submitted its $1 trillion stimulus request outlining priorities for the third phase of COVID-19 response legislation. That request can be found by clicking here.
  • Office of Management and Budget Acting Director Vought submitted a letter to Congressional appropriators with a request for $46 billion for agencies that have been stretched thin by the efforts to combat the outbreak. A copy of that letter can be found by clicking here.
  • Following President Trump’s emergency declaration pursuant to the Stafford Act, the U.S. Treasury Department and Internal Revenue Service (IRS) today issued guidance allowing all individual and other non-corporate tax filers to defer up to $1 million of federal income tax (including self-employment tax) payments due on April 15, 2020, until July 15, 2020, without penalties or interest.
